Wildfires and Socioeconomics

This map has been on my mind since created during week 5 of MAP671. I understand that economic access and social access to trash services causes many poor Kentuckians to burn their trash. Although the fire dept. are known arsonist of these wildfires, I suspect many of these wildfires stem from burning trash and hazardous waste. However, KY fish and wildlife state their investigations show it to be firefighters start these fires so they can be paid to put them out.

    Things to consider:

  • Trash pickups aren't subsidized in rural areas
  • Not every person has $30+ per month to pay for trash pickup
  • Tires and other hazardous wastes that cost a fee to recycle or have picked up are often burned by poor folk or dumped in ravines
  • Firefighters in poorer counties have been known to set fires so that they can be paid to extinguish them https://www.wkyt.com/content/news/Kentucky-firefighter-facing-arson-charges-570476821.html

    • SOLUTIONS TO REDUCE WILDFIRES IN KY

  • Subsidize trash pickups for poorer/rural communities
  • Increase free debris pickups from 1 time a year to 3 times a year
  • Punish arsonist firefighters more heavily
  • Combine county EMT and Fire Departments so that firefighters are double trained and less likely to set fires
  • reject private EMS contracts and support county funded EMS and FD
  • Support independent composters and trash haulers and their services-reduce certification barriers and fees
